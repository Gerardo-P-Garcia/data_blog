Gun Violence

11 Aug 2020, 21:13
R Markdown / plot / regression

Following the outbreak of COVID-19, the Black Lives Matter movement brought attention to police violence against the Black community and the people overall, with protests spanning the globe bringing additional scrutiny and evidence against long-established practices. A wealth of information has followed, including self-recorded videos and data, allowing us to connect systemic-level violence with the intensity of individual shootings that some may have dismissed as anecdotal. The police fatal-shooting data are from Ahsen Nazir’s data set uploaded to Kaggle, downloaded on 8/06/2020. The unedited data are accessible in full below, and are offered for transparency and access.

From the data, we see that men accounted for over 95% of reported shooting fatalities.

Just over 50% of victims were white, despite accounting for 75% of the US population. Meanwhile, 26.5% of victims were Black (13% of the population) and 18% were Hispanic (19%).

Using the 2019 US Census, we can control for the size of each race.
